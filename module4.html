Rings Of Responsibility

Child protection - whose responsibility?

In Module 2, we looked at the concept of the term “child,” child protection, and child safeguarding. Now, it will be helpful to discuss the roles of all those involved in child protection and safeguarding. This module will answer the question; who is responsible for child protection?

Protecting children at risk of abuse and neglect

The Family, Community, and institutions, both national and international, are responsible for protecting children from abuse and neglect.

Protecting children is everyone's responsibility, because:

  • It is a responsibility bestowed on all adult members of society by virtue of age, place, ability, and leadership responsibilities.

  • Every individual working with children is to protect all children based on the training, mandate, capacity acquired, and professional qualifications obtained. Part of such responsibilities is to timely report or act when a child is at risk.

  • As adults when we hear of concerns regarding harm to a child, we should report such concerns to someone who can help.

Protecting children is a shared responsibility, because:

  • It is a responsibility shared by diverse groups of professionals.

  • In Ondo state, the State Ministry of Women Affairs and Social Development (ODMWASD) and the Ondo State Agency Against Gender-Based Violence (OSAGBV) shared the responsibility for coordinating child protection

  • Decisions about children are never taken by one individual but always shared by an effective inter-agency working team.

Protecting children is the responsibility of immediate community, since:

  • The community has the cultural mandate to protect every child within their environment.

  • The leaders and key actors in a community setting are saddled with the obligation of protecting the younger generation within the community.

Child

The center ring has to do with the responsibilities a child has to him/herself such as understanding their rights as appropriate for their age.

Family

The first ring shows the family is the nearest to a child. The family has the immediate responsibility of protecting the child from harm or danger.

Community

The community stands for the child. This includes neighbours, family, friends, and caregivers. They must provide a safe space for the child in any environment.

Institutions

Institutions such as educational, health care, religious, recreational institutions have a duty to protect the child from harm or danger and report any child protection concern to the relevant authorities.

State and National

The three levels of government (local, state, and federal) have the duty to protect the child through provision of safe environment, enactment and enforcement of child friendly laws and policies.

International communities

Globally, countries have responsibilities to protect children through provisions of statutes and instruments for the protection of children.

Conclusion

The ring of responsibility shows how important it is for everyone to act within their ring to protect a child. The family, the community, and up to the international bodies have levels of actions that must be taken. Everyone must play their respective roles appropriately including YOU, the learner.

Parent’s and Guardian’s Affirmation

When I see something or hear something that could be harmful to a child, I will act and say something to prevent/stop it.
