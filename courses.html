Understanding Child Rights

This section defines who is a child and discusses the child’s rights as prescribed by various national and international laws and guidelines. Learners will be equipped with the knowledge of the basic rights for children as enshrined in the UN Charter for Child Rights and Nigeria’s Child's Rights Act (CRA), 2003 as well as the Ondo State Child’s Rights Law (ODCRL) 2007.

What Is Child Protection and Safeguarding?

Child protection includes the measures and structures put in place to prevent, respond to, and resolve abuse, neglect, exploitation, and violence against children in all settings.

Vulnerable Children or Children at Risk

Vulnerable children: According to the Orphans and Vulnerable Children’s National Plan of Action, vulnerable children can be said to be those who are most likely to be troubled and unable to cope with or participate in regular programs. In line with the social protection definition, vulnerable children are those who experience negative outcomes, such as loss of their education, indisposition, and malnutrition, at a higher rate than their peers.

Ring of Responsibility

What is Responsibility? This is bearing a duty or sense of accountability to an action, person, or thing. Protecting children at risk of abuse and neglect: The Family, Community, and institutions, both national and international, are responsible for protecting children from abuse and neglect.

Child Abuse, and Myths Related to Child Abuse

What is child Abuse? Child abuse is an act of taking an action or inaction that results in harming a child or puts a child at risk of harm. Child abuse can be physical, sexual, emotional, and psychological neglect.

Reporting Child Protection Concerns

In the earlier modules, you learnt about different types of child abuse. We also discussed the myths about child abuse. It is also identified that prompt reporting of child abuse is very important. If you see anything that can harm a child, say something. In this module you will learn about what, when, and where to report.
