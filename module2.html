What is Child Protection and Safeguarding?

At the end of this module, you should be able to understand child protection and child safeguarding.

Child Protection: What is it, and what does it involve?

Child protection includes the measures and structures put in place to prevent, respond to, and resolve abuse, neglect, exploitation, and violence against children in all settings.

Child protection involves the following:

  • Reducing Risks (or preventing abuse).

  • Making children ‘s rights a reality (or child rights).

  • Restoring hope and a dignified living (or well-being).

  • Creating an enabling environment (or protective environment).

  • Child Protection can also be summed up in two terms, as stated below:

“Prevention of” and “Response to” exploitation, abuse, neglect, harmful practices, and violence against children which form the basis of the Child Rights Law of Ondo State 2007 and Violence against Persons Prohibition Law (Laws of Ondo State) 2021.

What is safeguarding, and how does it apply to the child?

Safeguarding is a set of policies, procedures and practices employed to actively prevent harm, abuse, and distress.

All schools, public and private institutions, and organizations, as well as government ministries, departments and agencies within the community, Local government areas, or State should adopt safeguarding practices to ensure staff, teachers, caregivers, employees, volunteers, partners, vendors, and other representatives do not deliberately or unintentionally harm or abuse children.

What is the difference between child-Protection and child safeguarding?

While Child protection is specifically focused on preventive measures to ensure that all children are protected from intentional or unintentional acts that lead to the risk of or actual harm.

Child safeguarding is to create and keep a safe culture that is child-focused and community-driven through sustained and meaningful engagement with children, their families, communities, and all representatives.

Conclusion

A child needs to be protected by adults, who should ensure that the child is safe from harm. Measures and structures must be put in place to prevent, respond to, and resolve abuse, neglect, exploitation, and violence against children.

Parent’s and Guardian’s Affirmation

It is my responsibility to protect every child irrespective of the child’s color, sex, language, religion, political or other opinion, national, ethnic, or social origin, property, disability, birth, or other status. Every child has a right to be protected from abuse, therefore, I will protect them from harm.

Lessons Learnt

Parents, Teachers, community members, institutions, organisations, and caregivers should pay attention to their children's rights and ensure they are not exposed to harm and abuse.
