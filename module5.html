Child Abuse and Related Myths
What is child Abuse?

Child abuse is an act of taking an action or inaction that results in harming a child or puts a child at risk of harm. Child abuse can be physical, sexual, emotional, and psychological neglect. Examples of abuses are listed below:

  • Physical abuse of a child is when a parent or caregiver causes any non-accidental physical injury to a child.

  • Sexual abuse occurs when an adult uses a child for sexual purposes or involves a child in sexual acts. It can also occur when a child who is older or more powerful uses another child for sexual gratification or excitement.

  • Emotional abuse is when a parent or caregiver harms a child’s mental and social development or causes severe emotional harm. This is regarded as emotional abuse.

  • Neglect is when a parent or caregiver does not give the care, supervision, affection, and support needed for a child’s health, safety, and well-being. Neglect could be physical, emotional, medical, and educational.

Myths Related to Child Abuse

Myths are traditional stories, especially those concerning the early history of a people or explaining a natural or social occurrence. Myths typically involve supernatural beings or events.

Here are some of the common myths about child abuse.

Myth 1

“In my ethnic group a girl must be circumcised or else she will be promiscuous or may not be able to have a child.”

Fact

Female circumcision does not determine the promiscuousness of a girl or disturbs reproduction. It is very harmful to the health of the girl. The VAPP Law is against this practice and violators are liable to two years imprisonment.

Myth 2

“Sometimes, children’s behaviors require a harsh punishment.”

Fact

Such potentially harmful actions could damage a child permanently. Several other methods can be used to discipline a child. The CRL frowns at inflicting injuries on a child.

Myth 3

“Sometimes, children are to be blamed for their abuse”

Fact

A child needs the guidance of an adult and cannot take responsibility for their abuse.

Myth 4

“Children are not affected by domestic violence, if they do not see it happen”

Fact

A child does not need to see domestic violence to know it is happening and be affected.

Myth 5

“Child abuse only happens in poor families.”

Fact

No, it happens to both rich and poor families

Conclusion

Physical abuse, sexual abuse and other forms of abuse are obvious categories of child abuse, which can cause unexplained bruises such as scars, and broken bones. The VAPP Law defines physical abuse as an intentional act or threatened act of causing injury or trauma to, or the infliction of pain on another person by way of bodily contact such as slapping, hitting, kicking and or beating. Violence Against Persons Prohibition Law (VAPP)

Adult Affirmation

I will not abuse or allow any child to be sexually, physically, or emotionally abused. I will look out for the safety of every child and speak against myths that have caused harm and pain to children.

Parent’s and Guardian’s Affirmation

Every child need love, care, and sense of belonging. It is my duty to provide these.

Lessons Learnt

People in all institutions working with children should pay attention to and give listening ears to children.
