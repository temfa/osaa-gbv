Vulnerable children or children at risk

At the end of this module, you will be able to understand what makes a child vulnerable and at risk of being harmed. Examples will be given of children at risk of being harmed and how to prevent it.

Vulnerability

This is a term for the state of being unprotected or being exposed to physical or emotional dangers or attacks.

Vulnerable children

According to the Orphans and Vulnerable Children’s National Plan of Action, vulnerable children can be said to be those who are most likely to be troubled and unable to cope with or participate in regular programs. In line with the social protection definition, vulnerable children are those who experience negative outcomes, such as loss of their education, indisposition, and malnutrition, at a higher rate than their peers.

The Violence Against Persons Prohibition (VAPP) Law included children as part of the vulnerable groups because childhood refers to the period in which a human being is still developing mentally and physically and as such is dependent on adults for their overall care and wellbeing until they reach the age of maturity. This state of dependency exposes children to dangers, popularly known as vulnerability. The Violence Against Person’s (Prohibition) law frowns against abandonment of children or isolation of children from families and friends.

Factors promoting vulnerability.

  • Separation from parents, family members or a caring adult.

  • Violence or crises.

  • Abuse and exploitation.

  • Bad parenting.

Examples of vulnerable children or children at risk: children who are more vulnerable or at risk include but are not limited to.

  • Orphans.

  • Separated children.

  • Unaccompanied children.

  • Child-headed household.

  • Out of school children.

  • Children experiencing child labor.

  • Abandoned children.

  • Children with disability.

  • Children with complex health challenges.

  • Children internally displaced.

  • Children with terminally ill parents.

  • Children living with vulnerable parents.

Conclusion

Every child has the right to grow up in. an environment that supports their physical, psychological, social, and emotional development without any discrimination (CRA, Sec. 11(a-d), Sec. 16 (a-b)).

Parent’s and Guardian’s Affirmation

I will create, support, and sustain the right environment for every child to be at their best. It is my responsibility to protect and support every vulnerable or at-risk child.

Lessons Learnt

Vulnerable or at-risk children need special protection who may not have the same opportunities available to other children. They need to be supervised by adults, parents, caregivers, and relevant private and public institutions to ensure they are protected.
